## Card body

The three case studies below are windows into a single product I built from scratch — a mobile platform for the hourly workforce at Workstream.

When I joined, the product was a hiring and onboarding tool designed for managers on desktop. Workers didn't have a defined place in it at all. Over three years, working with a UX researcher, a PM, and a team of engineers, I helped establish the hourly worker as a distinct product persona and built out the operational layer that sits between a restaurant's business rules and the people carrying them out: a time clock, a scheduling and compliance engine, a labour cost visualisation tool, and a shared-device tablet experience.

Each case study zooms into a specific problem inside that system. This card is the view from outside it.

## Notes for implementation

The card should sit visually above or before the three case study cards in the selected works section — not as a fourth case study, but as a framing device. A few options depending on your layout:

- **Full-width banner** above the case study grid, with a different background colour to distinguish it from the clickable case study cards. Non-clickable, or links to your About page.
- **Left-anchored card** in a grid layout where the three case studies sit to the right, and this card spans the full height on the left.
- **Collapsed/expandable** — a single line ("These case studies are part of a larger system →") that expands on click. Lower visual weight, still adds context.

Whichever layout you choose, the visual distinction between this card and the case study cards matters — it should read as context, not content. Different background, no hover state, or a clearly different typographic treatment will do it.
